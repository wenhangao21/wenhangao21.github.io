The ultimate goal of these blogs is to make research more accessible, especially in a landscape where much of the shared content (published papers, oral presentations, etc.) leans toward low-entropy, story-heavy narratives. My blogs aim to explore fundamental concepts and underlying mechanisms, sharing my understanding in the simplest way possible.

These blogs are intended to (1) simplify abstract concepts for newcomers and (2) provide the broader ML/DL community with a quick overview of my research areas. When appropriate, coding examples are included to help illustrate key concepts. The code is intentionally kept much simpler than complex libraries, yet it captures the essential functionalities needed to grasp the underlying ideas, allowing anyone to follow each line and run experiments within minutes on almost any laptop, desktop, or Google Colab (except for blogs involving large language models, which require more computational resources).
